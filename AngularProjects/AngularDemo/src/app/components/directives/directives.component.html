DIRECTIVES

In Angular, we have access to something called a "directive". Using directives allows us to invoke Angular fucntionality without breaking our frontend. Angular directives are used within our template/view. There are several types of directives, two of them being "Structural Directives" and "Attribute DIrectives"

Structural Directives

A structural directive allows us to change the structure of a template. This entails, for instance, adding or removing elements from our view. Structural directives provide a conenient way of performing DOM manipulation. We denote that we are using structural directive by using the "*" symbol.

*ngIf

ngIf is a structural directive which is used in conjuction with some boolean expression/value in our model. If that expression/value should evaluate to false, the element on which you are using ngIf will be removed from the DOM. Else, it will be placed on the DOM.
